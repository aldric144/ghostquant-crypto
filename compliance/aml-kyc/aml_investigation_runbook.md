# AML Investigation Runbook

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** December 2025  
**Classification:** Confidential - Internal Use Only

---


This runbook provides step-by-step procedures for investigating suspicious activities detected by GhostQuant™ intelligence engines. It ensures consistent, thorough, and well-documented investigations that support SAR filing decisions and regulatory examinations.

---



---



**Sources:**
- UltraFusion AI™ transaction monitoring alerts
- Actor Profiler™ behavioral alerts
- Hydra™ network alerts
- Oracle Eye™ document fraud alerts
- Sanctions screening matches
- PEP screening matches
- Manual referrals

**Alert Information:**
- Alert ID and timestamp
- Rule or engine that generated alert
- Severity level (Critical, High, Medium, Low)
- Entity or transaction details
- Evidence summary

---


**Triage Criteria:**
- Severity level
- Alert type
- Customer risk score
- Transaction amount
- Regulatory deadlines

**Triage Decisions:**
- **Immediate Investigation:** Critical alerts, sanctions matches, high-value transactions
- **Priority Investigation:** High severity, PEP matches, structuring patterns
- **Standard Investigation:** Medium severity, behavioral anomalies
- **Batch Investigation:** Low severity, minor anomalies

**Assignment:**
- Critical/High: Senior AML Analyst
- Medium: AML Analyst
- Low: Junior AML Analyst

**SLA:**
- Critical: Begin within 4 hours
- High: Begin within 24 hours
- Medium: Begin within 72 hours
- Low: Begin within 7 days

---


**Case Information:**
- Unique case ID
- Alert ID(s) linked
- Assigned investigator
- Priority level
- Creation timestamp
- Expected completion date
- Case status (Open, In Progress, Escalated, Closed)

**Documentation:**
- All case information logged to Genesis Archive™
- Case file created in case management system
- Investigation checklist generated

---



**Review Activities:**
- Review alert details and evidence
- Understand triggering rule or pattern
- Review entity/transaction information
- Identify additional related alerts
- Assess preliminary risk level

**Questions to Answer:**
- What triggered the alert?
- Is this a true positive or false positive?
- What is the potential money laundering risk?
- Are there other related alerts or activities?
- Is immediate action required?

**Assessment Questions:**

5. Investigator adds human judgment and context
6. Final narrative approved

**Immediate Escalation to AML Officer:**

**Review Activities:**

- Review narrative

**Possible Decisions:**
- File SAR
- Do not file SAR (document rationale)
- Request additional investigation
- Escalate to senior management
- Notify law enforcement
- Terminate relationship
- Enhance monitoring
- Close case

---


**SAR Filing Criteria:**
- Transactions involving $5,000+ with known or suspected criminal activity
- Transactions designed to evade BSA requirements
- Transactions with no apparent lawful purpose
- Transactions involving proceeds of illegal activity
- Transactions to facilitate criminal activity

**SAR Filing Process:**
1. AML Officer approves SAR filing
2. SAR narrative finalized
3. Supporting documentation compiled
4. SAR filed via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System
5. SAR filing logged to Genesis Archive™
6. Customer monitoring enhanced
7. Follow-up actions implemented

**SAR Deadline:** 30 days from initial detection
